Wildlife of Sri Lanka includes its flora and fauna and their natural habitats. Sri Lanka has probably the most elevated rates of organic endemism (16% of the fauna and 23% of blooming plants are endemic) on the planet. Consequently, Sri Lanka takes up a great deal of pride to give an ideal climate to preserve and secure it's common fortunes. One such measure is the creation and upkeep of the Dehiwala Zoo

The National Zoological Gardens of Sri Lanka is a zoological nursery arranged in Dehiwala, Sri Lanka, which was established in 1936. Its rambling zones are host to an assortment of creatures and birds. The zoo displays creatures yet in addition puts an accentuation on creature protection and government assistance, and training. The Dehiwala National Zoo, which has a striking assortment of outlandish and native fauna, is probably the most established zoo in Asia.

At present, Dehiwala Zoo houses 72 types of Mammals, 65 types of Birds, 31 types of Reptiles, 89 types of Fish, 03 groups of Amphibians and 30 types of Butterflies and so forth The total number of creatures shifts from 2500-3000 because of rearing and free living settlements of birds.

NEW ARRIVALS!

We the Dehiwala Zoo family are excited to introduce you all to some of the new arrivals who will be staying with us.

  1. Lana the Lionness

    Lana was born on 10th September 2001 and flew down to Sri Lanka all the way from Africa. She is lighter in colour and weighs about 196kg. Lana loves scent-based enrichment, including herbs and hessian sacks and the odd egg and fish treat. Lana loves a good nap as she spends up to 20 hours of the day sleeping or resting. She can be found lying on her back with her feet up or taking a snooze in the shade.

  2. Maxy the Python

    Maxy is a 25 year old male ball python donated to the zoo by a private donor. Ball pythons are named after their natural defensive behaviors. They tend to curl up into a ball with their head toward the center of the ball when threatened. With proper socialization, they can be easily handled and are very docile. They are also called the “royal python” because ancient royalty wore them as jewelry. No two ball pythons are alike. They all have their own beautiful and unique patterns. So we can safely say his spots are far different from our older python "Leela" who has been with us for 3 years.

  3. Vega the Sumatran Elephant

    Vega was a rescue Elephant who was under the care of the Pinnawala Rehabillitation Elephant Sanctuary after he was injured by poachers in 2016. However, he is all healed up now and transfered to us where he can live safely away from the harm of any poachers. He is 40 year's old and loves to have long baths where he gets a nice back scrub from our care takers.

  4. Zhang the White Tiger

    Zhang was donated to us by the Chineese National Zoo and we consider ourselves very lucky to have the opportunity to take care of him as The White Tiger is one of the rarer relatives of the big cats. He is 1.5 years old so he still is relatively young so he loves his daily bottle of milk, but that doesn't stop him from tearing into his lunch which is an assortment of meat.

  5. Seetha the Albino Indian Cobra

    Last but definietly not the least we are lucky to now home Seetha the rare Albino Indian Cobra. Albino cobras are among 10 of the rarest Albino animals in the world so you can imagine how honored the Dehiwala Zoo is to have her. Other than the fact that she has Albinism she is in all sorts a normal Cobra so she is a fiesty girl who loves her personal space.
